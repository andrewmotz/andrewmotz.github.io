2.4.2 - CSS3

A) Use CSS3 to format the appearance of paragraphs, lists, headers, background images, tables, forms.

Example of CSS on a form. Border and spacing was done is CSS.

B) Use CSS3 contextual selectors (e1, e2, e3; e f; e > f; e + f) and at least two attribute selectors, excluding class and id attribute selectors.

The nav bar uses "ul > a:nth-of-type(5)" to add a gray border on right side of the last <a>. The other ones have only a border on the left.

C) Use of CSS3 styles for color (RGBA & HSLA).

For the rgb I used "rgba(255,255,255, .80)" for a white and semi transparent background for the article of each page. Done in base_styles.css on line: 8.

For hsla I set the nav to "hsla(0, 100%, 100%, 0.9)". Done in base_styles.css on line: 49.

D) Use of dynamic pseudo-classes to highlight hyperlinks states of link, visited, active, hover; focus and two structural pseudo-classes: first-of-type and last-of-type.

The nav bar links are styled in base_styles.css line: 77 to 94. link, active, visited and hover are used.

The nav hamburger uses structural pseudo-classes in base_styles.css line 214 to 233.

E) Use of CSS3 background styles (background size, background-clip, background-origin, padding, margins, borders, use of an appropriate border style, use of rounded corners.

The article of all the pages uses background properties such as border-radius.

The background images of all the pages use "background: url(X) no-repeat fixed center;" and "background-size: cover;". index_style.css will have this in the top of the file.
